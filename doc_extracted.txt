Swastik Chatting Platform  
Why Swastik Chatting Website is Important  
 A Digital Home for University Students  
In today’s hyperconnected world, students are constantly seeking platforms that allow 
them to collaborate, express, socialize, and stay informed — all in one place. However, 
most existing platforms like WhatsApp, Instagram, and Discord are either too broad, too 
personal, or lack campus -specific integration.  
There is a growing need for a university -exclusive platform that blends:  
• The connectivity of a messenger app  
• The engagement of a social network  
• The functionality of a campus management system  
That’s where Swastik Chatting Website comes in.  
 
The Problem It Solves  
1.  Lack of Focused Campus Communication:  
Existing social platforms are not tailored for students to talk about university 
events, clubs, and classroom activities in a structured and safe environment.  
2.  Privacy Issues in General Platforms:  
Many mainstream apps expose users to unwanted attention or data 
vulnerabilities. Students need a platform where they control their visibility and 
interactions . 
3.  Missing Campus Culture Online:  
From anonymous confessions to event promotions, the offline spirit of campus 
life is not captured by general apps. Swastik Chatting brings these elements to 
life virtually.  
4. Overcrowded Platforms:  
Traditional social media is flooded with noise, ads, and distractions. Students 
require a dedicated space  for academic, social, and personal development — 
not just entertainment.  
 
Why Swastik Chatting Website Matters  
• Built for Students, Not General Users:  
Every feature — from anonymous confessions to exclusive club access — is 
created specifically with students’ lives and challenges in mind.  
• Verified & Safe:  
With university email verification, only genuine students get access — building a 
trustworthy and respectful environment . 
•  All-in-One Solution:  
No need to juggle multiple platforms — chat, events, study groups, anonymous 
confessions, and club management are all here.  
•  Preserves the Fun Side of College:  
It’s not just about utility — fun and expression are at the heart of the platform 
with memes, trends, fame boards, and social discovery.  
 
Social Media Platform for Students – Feature Documentation  
1. Student Verification and Account Creation  
Before accessing the platform, every student is required to verify their identity using a 
valid university -issued email ID. Only after successful email verification will the student 
be allowed to create an account on the platform. This ensures that only genuine 
students from r ecognized universities can participate, maintaining a secure and 
academic -oriented environment.  
2. Automatic Group Enrollment on Registration  
Once a student successfully creates an account:  
• The user will be automatically added to a Welcome Group , which is pre -created 
by the developer.  
• This group acts as a common chat space for all verified users across different 
universities.  
• Every new user who registers is automatically joined into this group to encourage 
initial engagement and interaction.  
• This group is developer -operated and is not modifiable by users.  
• To differentiate between student of different universities , a badge will be issued 
to every user of that universities where they belong and that badge will be visible 
beside their username  
3. University -Specific Groups  
In addition to the Welcome Group:  
• A dedicated group for each university  is pre-built and maintained by the 
developers.  
• These groups are only visible and accessible to students from the respective 
universities.  
• Upon registration, a user is automatically enrolled in the university -specific 
group based on their verified email domain.  
• Users from other universities will not be able to see or join university -specific 
groups that do not correspond to their domain.  
4. Group Creation by Users  
Verified users can create their own groups on the platform. There are three types of 
groups:  
a) Public Groups  
• Any student from any university can view and join public groups.  
• These groups are listed in the group discovery section for all users.  
b) Private Groups  
• These groups are not visible in the group discovery section.  
• Only users who have the group password (shared by the creator) can join.  
• Suitable for friends or closed communities.  
c) Semi-Public Groups  
• These groups are visible only to students from a specific university, as defined by 
the group creator during group setup.  
• Students from other universities will neither see nor join these groups.  
• Ideal for inter -class or inter -department collaboration within a university.  
5. Group Management and Roles  
Groups (other than the Welcome Group and university -specific groups) are operated 
and managed by users. The group creator becomes the Admin and can delegate 
additional roles:  
a) Admin  
• Creator of the group.  
• Has full control over the group including editing name, description, permissions, 
and assigning staff roles.  
b) Co-Admin  
• Can only be assigned by the Admin.  
• Permissions include:  
o Ban or unban users  
o Change group name and description  
o Assign or remove Moderators  
o View group logs  
• Cannot assign or remove other Co -Admins.  
c) Moderator (Mod)  
• Assigned by Admin or Co -Admin.  
• Permissions include:  
o Ban or unban users  
o View group logs  
• Cannot assign or remove other Moderators or Co -Admins.  
• Cannot change group metadata.  
6. Group Logs  
Each group has a private Logs section accessible only to the staff members (Admins, 
Co-Admins, and Moderators). The log records include:  
• Deleted messages (along with user and deleter identity)  
• Banned or unbanned user actions (along with the staff identity who performed 
the action)  
This log system ensures transparency and accountability in group moderation and 
management.  
7. Developer -Controlled Groups  
• The Welcome Group  and University -Specific Groups  are exclusively controlled 
and operated by the platform developers.  
• No students or staff members have moderation rights in these groups.  
• Only developers can edit content, remove users, or configure settings in these 
groups.  
8. Post System  
The platform provides students with a dynamic post creation system that caters to both 
personal and public engagement. Users can choose between two visibility modes for 
each post:  
a) Public Post  
• A public post is visible to all users  on the platform, regardless of whether they 
follow the poster.  
• Ideal for sharing general thoughts, achievements, announcements, or 
discussions that may be useful to the larger community.  
• These posts appear in the general feed and are indexed for visibility.  
b) Private Post  
• A private post is only visible to users who follow the poster.  
• Intended for personal sharing , such as day -to-day experiences, opinions, or 
updates meant for a selective audience.  
• Helps maintain a sense of privacy and controlled visibility.  
• Users will be presented with the visibility selection prompt  (public/private) 
every time they create a new post.  
This dual posting mechanism encourages users to express themselves freely while 
maintaining control over who can see their content.  
9. Discussion Segment  
The platform contains a dedicated Discussion Section , distinct from the main post 
feed, designed to promote professional and academic engagement.  
Key Characteristics:  
• Users can share ideas, opinions, achievements, current affairs insights, or 
start topic-based discussions . 
• Not intended for personal media sharing such as selfies or casual content.  
• Posts in this section are treated with more seriousness and are structured to 
encourage meaningful interaction and collaboration.  
This segment supports the platform’s academic intent and allows students to network 
intellectually beyond casual interactions.  
 
10. Anonymous Discussion  
The platform includes a built -in Anonymous Discussion  segment accessible directly 
from the main dashboard . This segment is not a part of the general group list , nor 
does it require users to join it manually. It is a special, always -available discussion area 
meant for open, candid conversations.  
Key Characteristics:  
• Functions like a dedicated group , but with a major difference: all user identities 
are fully hidden . 
• Any message posted within this segment will not display the user's name or ID . 
Instead, each message will be labeled under a generic tag such as 
“Anonymous” . 
• No user profile, avatar, or traceable information will be attached to any message.  
• This segment is prominently displayed on the dashboard under the label 
"Anonymous Discussion"  and users can access it directly by clicking the 
section.  
Purpose:  
• Allows students to freely express opinions , seek help, or share sensitive 
concerns without fear of judgment.  
• Suitable for discussing personal experiences, mental health topics, 
institutional feedback , or controversial subjects.  
• Maintains a safe and inclusive environment  where users feel comfortable 
speaking up.  
This anonymous interaction channel is tightly moderated internally for content safety, 
ensuring it remains a respectful and meaningful space.  
11. User Relationship System  
The platform distinguishes between professional engagement  and personal 
interaction  by providing two different user connection mechanisms:  
a) Follow System  
• A user may follow another user to receive updates on their posts and 
discussions . 
• Following is one-way and does not require mutual consent.  
• It does not enable chatting  or any personal interaction.  
• Designed for students who wish to stay informed  about others’ contributions in 
a professional or academic context.  
b) Friend System  
• Enables mutual connection  between two users.  
• A friend request must be accepted  for the connection to be established.  
• Only friends can engage in direct personal chats . 
• Intended for private interaction , collaboration, and informal communication.  
This bifurcated system clearly separates professional visibility from private 
communication, offering users full control over how they wish to engage with others on 
the platform.  
 
12. Random Chat (One -on-One Anonymous Conversations)  
The platform includes a dedicated Random Chat  segment that allows students to 
engage in one-on-one conversations  with peers selected through an internal 
algorithm. This feature is designed to foster spontaneous interaction , idea exchange , 
and community building  in an anonymous and respectful environment.  
Key Characteristics:  
• Each session connects two students randomly  and anonymously.  
• No usernames, profile pictures, or identifying information  are displayed 
during the conversation.  
• Each participant is displayed under a generic label such as “Anonymous” . 
• The goal is to facilitate open-minded dialogue , exchange of thoughts, and 
understanding of diverse perspectives without preconceived judgments.  
Post-Conversation Engagement:  
• If both users find the conversation meaningful  or believe the interaction may be 
professionally or personally valuable , they have the option to add each other 
as friends . 
• An “Add Friend”  button is available beside the “Anonymous” label at the top 
during the chat.  
• A friend connection can only be established if both users mutually accept  the 
friend request, maintaining full consent.  
Purpose:  
• Encourages cross-university collaboration , spontaneous networking, and 
communication skill development.  
• Offers a unique space where students can practice expression , explore 
different viewpoints , or even discuss academics informally without identity 
concerns.  
• Maintains a balance of anonymity , safety, and optional connectivity  after 
mutual interest.  
This segment is intentionally structured to avoid superficial engagement  and instead 
promote meaningful, respectful, and idea -centric interaction among the student 
community.  
 
13. Notification Segment  
The platform includes a dedicated Notification Segment  designed to keep students 
informed about relevant academic, cultural, and technical activities happening within 
their university ecosystem and beyond. This segment acts as a centralized event 
bulletin for timely updates and seamless participation.  
Key Characteristics:  
• All users receive real-time notifications  about:  
o University events  (seminars, festivals, guest lectures, etc.)  
o Club activities  (student-run initiatives, recruitment drives, workshops)  
o Hackathons and tech events  
• Notifications are displayed in a structured, chronological feed within the 
Notifications  segment on the dashboard.  
Ticketing and Event Participation:  
• Users can view event details  directly through the notification, including date, 
time, venue (online/offline), and description.  
• If the event requires registration or entry passes, students can secure tickets 
directly through the platform  using the in -built ticketing system.  
• Events marked as “Open for All” will be visible to all registered users , regardless 
of their university.  
• Events that are restricted to a specific university  will be shown only to 
students from that university , ensuring relevance and data privacy.  
Purpose:  
• Encourages active student involvement  in university life by simplifying access 
to information.  
• Supports academic growth and skill -building by promoting participation in 
competitions, hackathons, and community discussions.  
• Acts as a bridge between students and university clubs or organizers , 
enabling a more connected, engaged, and updated campus environment.  
This segment ensures that students remain informed and never miss out on 
opportunities that contribute to their personal, professional, and academic growth.  
 
14. Academic Resources Section  
The platform provides a structured Academic Resources Section  to promote 
collaborative learning and content sharing among students. This segment enables 
users to access and contribute useful educational material including class notes, 
previous year question papers (PYQs), reference documents, and more.  
Key Features:  
• Upload System:  Students can upload various academic resources in categories 
like Notes, PYQs, Assignments, and Reference PDFs.  
• Content Filtering:  A filter system allows users to view materials by subject, 
semester, department, and resource type.  
• Verification Workflow:  Uploaded files undergo a verification process by the 
internal review team before becoming publicly visible to ensure content quality 
and academic integrity.  
• Contributor Recognition:  The system tracks student contributions and awards 
monthly badges to the most helpful users, encouraging consistent participation.  
Purpose:  
• Promotes peer learning and academic collaboration across batches and 
departments.  
• Creates a centralized and easily accessible repository for verified study 
materials.  
• Motivates users to contribute valuable content by recognizing their efforts.  
 
15. Weekly Polls & Quizzes  
To foster engagement and challenge users intellectually, the platform includes a Polls & 
Quizzes Segment  similar to platforms like LeetCode.  
Features:  
• Weekly Contests:  Users participate in time -bound quizzes or opinion -based 
polls every week.  
• Live Leaderboard:  A dynamic leaderboard updates in real -time, displaying top 
scorers of the week.  
• Question Format:  MCQs, subjective -type responses, and image/audio -based 
questions are supported.  
• Timed Mode:  Quizzes are conducted in timed sessions with anti -cheating 
mechanisms.  
Badges & Rewards:  
• Top performers receive the “Sharp Mind”  badge for the week.  
• Performance analytics are shown on the user’s profile to track progress over 
time.  
 
16. AI-Based Content and Group Suggestions  
An AI Recommendation System  is implemented to personalize user experiences 
across the platform based on interests, activities, and interactions.  
Functionality:  
• Post Suggestions:  AI curates feed content including discussion threads, 
academic resources, or meme posts aligned with user behavior.  
• Group Discovery:  The system suggests public and private groups that match the 
user’s interests, academic stream, or frequently viewed topics.  
• Adaptive Learning:  The recommendation engine evolves over time, improving 
suggestion accuracy as user interaction increases.  
 
17. Campus Moderation and Decentralized Group Management  
To balance freedom and order within student interactions, a decentralized moderation 
model is adopted.  
Moderator System:  
• Campus Representatives:  Each university will have one student moderator per 
academic year. These moderators manage reported users, ensure community 
guidelines, and monitor spam activity.  
• Selection Process:  
o Stage 1 – AI Shortlisting:  Candidates are filtered automatically based on 
platform behavior, activeness, and overall contribution.  
o Stage 2 – Human Interview:  Shortlisted candidates undergo a virtual 
interview before final selection.  
Privileges:  
• Manage reported users and posts within their campus.  
• Moderate university -specific groups and discussion forums.  
• Assist in promoting academic and event -related content to their peers.  
 
18. Badge System  
To recognize student contributions and achievements, a structured Badge System  is 
implemented:  
1. Resource Champion  – Awarded to top contributors in the Academic Resources 
section.  
2. Community Leader  – For students maintaining the most active self -created 
groups.  
3. Campus Star  – Granted to users with high participation across chats, 
discussions, and events.  
4. Sharp Mind  – For weekly toppers in polls and quizzes (badge rotates weekly).  
5. Verification Badges  – Designated for Admins, Co -admins, Moderators, and 
Campus Representatives for authenticity.
